DENT-DDSP: Data-Efficient Noisy speech generaTor using differentiable digital signal processors for explicit distortion modelling and noise-robust speech recognitio

Guo Zixun
Chng Eng Siong

Nanyang Technological University, Singapore

Explicit distortion modelling using DDSP to simulate air traffic control(ATC) noisy speech

Explicit distortion modeling is able to enhance automatic speech recognition(ASR) systems under noisy conditions. In this paper, we propose a fully explainable model DENT to simulate VHF/UHF transmitted data(e.g. air traffic control speech). DENT stands for data-efficient noisy speech generator and utilizes novel differentiable digital processing(DDSP) components as backends. The novel and trainable DDSP components proposed are waveshaper and computationally-efficient dynamic range compressor(DRC) which can be easily intergrated to other DDSP models. DENT only requires 10-seconds of training data which is 60 time less compare to SimuGAN[1], the current state-of-the-art model that utilizes GAN to simulate VHF/UHF transmitted data. Yet, DENT outperforms Simu- GAN in terms of simulation quality and generalization ability on unseen data. Simulated data from DENT are utilized to train noisy-robust ASR systems and outperform other strong baseline model in terms of word error rate(WER).
